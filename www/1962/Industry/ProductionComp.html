How Does Production Company Insurance Work?

From mini-series to full-fledged feature films, there are many ways that the companies behind a film or TV production can benefit from an insurance policy.

Production company insurance can be purchased either as an annual plan or a short-term policy. Annual coverage is better suited to companies juggling multiple productions throughout the year, as well as production houses that rent out their own equipment to freelancers or other independent entities. Short-term insurance policies are purchased on a project-by-project basis, starting with just one day of coverage.

DICE production insurance is specifically intended for documentaries, industrial, commercial, and educational films, though music videos and short features can also fall under this type of policy. This type of policy is a form of pre-set coverage for your office and all your productions on a one-year team. It can cover any productions that unfold within that year as long as their individual budgets do not exceed $150,000. This type of policy allows you to choose your locations and rent equipment as you see fit, and the policy will accommodate your choices as they roll in.

Onset injuries, loss or damage of property and equipment, worker’s compensation, and professional errors can all be covered under a production company insurance policy. Consult with Divrisk to understand which of these comprehensive plans suits you best.

What Does Production Company Insurance Cover?

Life on-set can be unpredictable. With a huge number of moving parts, from people to equipment and everything in between, it is essential for your production house to be covered in the event of an incident.

General: The number of people working on a film or television set can be overwhelming. If a third party should sustain an injury while on the job, your insurance policy can cover their medical expenses. The same type of coverage should extend to any rented or non-owned assets or property that may incur damage during production.

Staff: Your employees dedicate large quantities of their time and creativity to each production, and should receive the coverage they deserve for their hard work. If any of your staff gets injured or is unable to continue working, your production insurance should take care of their expenses related to treatment and recovery.

Property: The specialized equipment and tools that belong to your production company are essential to its operation, and must be protected in the event of damage. Property insurance can cover your negatives, cameras, office contents, sets, wardrobe, and more.

Vehicle: With all the running around involved in bringing a production to life, you need to think ahead: what if the company car was damaged in a collision? Or worse, what if the driver or passenger was injured? Commercial auto insurance can help defer the cost of car repairs and any ensuing medical expenses.

What Commercial Insurance Policy Do You Need?

In such a creative industry, no two productions will ever be the same. A good film production insurance policy will tend to your unique situation. An annual policy must be tailored to the company’s overall needs, while a short-term policy should take care to ensure proper coverage for each of your productions on a case-by-case basis.

If you damage a third party’s property at any point during production, general liability insurance can help you cover the cost of replacing or repairing the damage. For example, if you rent a specialized camera and scratch the lens by accident, or if you damage the floor moving heavy equipment into a temporary filming location.

General can also help cover any legal and medical bodily harm costs for claims filed by third parties, such as an on-set helper slipping and falling while on the clock. If that individual decides to sue your company, general liability can also help cover the legal fees resulting from the suit.

In the entertainment industry, commercial vehicles are typically covered under general liability. If your company car(s) experience(s) a collision, your insurance can help cover any repair costs associated with the accident, as well as medical expenses if the driver or passenger(s) sustained any injuries.

In order to acquire a permit to film your production, many cities and/or counties require you to have purchased general liability insurance beforehand.

Workers’ Compensation

If one of your employees sustains an injury while working, workers’ compensation policies are intended to help cover associated medical costs. This type of coverage is mandated at a state level to varying degrees, and many business that employ at least one person are required to purchase it.

Workers’ comp can provide funds for your staff member to cover the cost of their expenses for medical treatment and recovery. If a workplace injury is escalated to a lawsuit by the employee, or if someone chooses to sue after sustaining an injury that is not covered by your regular policy, workers’ compensation can help cover fees related to legal proceedings.

Situations can include: a piece of equipment falling on a staff member, an employee injuring their back lifting heavy objects, or a slip-and-fall injury.

Property Liability

For production companies in particular, much of the equipment and assets in your possession comes with a very high price tag. With property liability insurance, not only will the contents of your office be protected, but it can also protect invaluable items including cameras, film negatives, props and sets, grip and lighting gear, and more.

Errors and Omissions

This type of policy is mandatory for any production that is intended to be distributed for profit. When it comes to intellectual and creative property, it is essential for your production company to be protected against claims related to supposed unauthorized use of titles, format, ideas, characters, and plots , as well as plagiarism and unfair competition. This type of policy can also cover libel, slander, defamation of character, or invasion of privacy, and legal fees related to any legal suits related to the above.

How Much Does production company insurance cost?

For short-term or annual insurance, you should be budgeting at least a few thousand dollars to put towards your policy. As a rule of thumb, you should set aside approximately 2.5% of your production’s budget for your insurance.

Policies typically start at $1 million of coverage, however if your location is open to the public or selling tickets, it may require an umbrella policy of $5 million.
