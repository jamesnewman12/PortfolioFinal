Claim Example

You own a restaurant. You have been open for a few months, and your in-house staff is still learning the ropes. One evening, the restaurant is packed and orders for the grilled salmon are raining down upon the kitchen. The fish you currently have in stock is only expired by a day or two, so you roll the dice and serve it up.

A couple of diners get sick and slap you with a lawsuit, forcing your restaurant to shut down for an indeterminate amount of time while the legal process is underway. Small business insurance is able to tackle this issue from many angles.

Firstly, general liability protects you in the event of a third party filing a complaint against your business related to bodily harm or sickness. Meanwhile, errors and omissions is directly related to legal situations arising from a failure to provide your service in a way that complies with professional standards, should the lawsuit hold water. Finally, business income liability will help defer the cost of your restaurant shutting down, including paying your employees and supplementing some of your lost income.
